How Advanced Production Notes Should Stay Optional

Advanced Production exists to improve a Scene, not to make the basic workflow harder. This guide explains why it stays folded until the creator wants more detail.

Basic production must work first

Every Scene should be usable with the visible fields alone. Narration, Scene Focus, Image Prompt, music keywords, and Editing Guide must be enough to produce the moment.

Advanced Production is for creators who want extra refinement after that basic structure is clear.

Folded notes protect the workflow

Keeping advanced notes collapsed prevents the page from overwhelming beginners. It also helps experienced creators move quickly through the core Scene.

The button makes extra detail available without pushing it into the main production path.

Motion and sound are not always needed

Some Scenes benefit from motion prompts, sound effects, or transition notes. Others are stronger when they stay quiet and still.

Optional information should match the Scene instead of repeating the same suggestion everywhere.

Advanced does not mean complicated

Advanced Production should remain practical. It may describe a slow push-in, a faint room tone, or a simple color direction.

The goal is better control, not professional complexity.
