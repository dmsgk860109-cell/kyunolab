How to Use Scene Workspaces Without Losing the Story

Scene workspaces are built for making video, but each Scene still belongs to a larger story. This guide explains how to follow the workflow without breaking the narrative.

Guide Map

  1. Treat each Scene as a work unit
  2. Keep the Scene Role visible
  3. Move through the fields in order
  4. Check the transition before moving on

Treat each Scene as a work unit

A Scene is the smallest production unit in the Creator Library. It gathers narration, visual focus, prompts, music direction, and editing guidance in one place.

This prevents the creator from jumping between unrelated sections while building a single moment of the video.

Keep the Scene Role visible

Scene Role explains what the moment does inside the video. It may hook the viewer, introduce a legend, build doubt, present evidence, or leave a final question.

Before producing the assets, check the role. It keeps the Scene from becoming a pretty image with no narrative job.

Move through the fields in order

Start with the narration. Then read the voice direction and Scene Focus before copying the image prompt.

That order matters because the image should support the spoken story, not pull the video into a different mood.

Check the transition before moving on

A Scene is not finished when the image is created. It is finished when the viewer can move naturally from this moment to the next one.

Use the Editing Guide and optional advanced notes to make the end of the Scene feel connected to the next beat.
