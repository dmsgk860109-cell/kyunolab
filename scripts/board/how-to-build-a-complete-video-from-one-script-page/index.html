How to Build a Complete Video From One Script Page

A Creator Library page is designed to carry the creator from understanding the story to assembling the final video. This guide explains the intended flow.

Guide Map

  1. Begin with Story
  2. Prepare the basic assets

  4. Finish with the reference area

Begin with Story

Start by reading Story Summary and Story Information. This gives the topic, mood, core motif, setting, and production difficulty before any assets are made.

The first step is not copying a prompt. The first step is knowing what kind of video the story wants to become.

Prepare the basic assets

Creator Toolkit shows the asset types to prepare: images, narration, background music, editing, and optional motion.

Production Workflow then turns that preparation into a simple order of work.

Create Scene by Scene

Choose Long-form Creator or Short-form Creator. Then work through the Scenes in order.

Each Scene gives the narration first, then the visual and editing material needed to produce that moment.

Finish with the reference area

After production, use the Finish area for the original story, usage note, and related script paths.

The page is complete when the creator can understand the source, make the video, and know where the material came from.
