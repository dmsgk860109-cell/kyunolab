Why Story Summary Comes Before Production Assets

The Creator Library is a production space, but it still begins with the story. This order keeps the video grounded before prompts and editing choices begin.

Production starts with understanding

A video can only stay coherent if the creator understands the story first. Story Summary gives the subject, mood, conflict, and source boundary before any assets are created.

This prevents the production from chasing visuals that feel dramatic but do not match the record.

Story Information sets the production frame

Story Information works like a quick production brief. It identifies genre, motif, setting, mood, difficulty, and recommended length.

Those details guide the creator's pacing and tone before the first Scene begins.

Shared context prevents repetition

Long-form and Short-form both use the same Story area. That keeps common information from being repeated in two different places.

The production sections can then focus on what each format needs to make the video work.

Assets come after the record is clear

Image prompts, music keywords, and editing notes make better sense after the creator knows what the story is doing.

The Story area is not a delay. It is the anchor that keeps the production from drifting away from the mystery.
