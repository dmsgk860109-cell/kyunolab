Quick Answer

In brief, Selkies are seal people in Scottish, Orcadian, and North Atlantic folklore. They can remove their seal skins, appear in human form, and sometimes become trapped on land when the skin is hidden. The best-known stories often involve marriage, longing, and return to the sea. The selkie may be read as a figure of divided belonging rather than a simple monster.

The main movement is clear: In many versions, a human finds or steals a selkie’s seal skin. Without the skin, the selkie cannot return to the sea and may live as a spouse on land. Some versions focus on selkie wives. Others describe male selkies who visit human women. The emotional center changes, but the skin remains the key object.
