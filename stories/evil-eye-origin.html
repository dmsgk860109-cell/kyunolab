Quick Answer

In brief, The evil eye is the belief that a harmful or envious glance can bring misfortune. Many cultures developed charms, gestures, or sayings to protect against it. The origin matters because the belief gives social emotion a visible force. Envy becomes something that can move through a look.

The main movement is clear: Across many traditions, a glance shaped by envy or malice is believed to cause harm. The danger may fall on children, animals, success, beauty, or good fortune. Some traditions focus on deliberate envy. Others treat the harmful glance as accidental, especially when praise is not balanced by a protective phrase.
