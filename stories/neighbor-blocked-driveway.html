FICTIONAL STORY · NEIGHBOR DISPUTES

Neighbor Blocked The Driveway For Revenge, Then Learned What Private Property Means

He called it a lesson. The tow company called it Tuesday.

Published July 1, 2026 · 7 min read

Fiction notice: This story is fictional and written for entertainment.

Mr. Vale believed the curb in front of three houses belonged to him because he had lived on the street the longest. When a new family parked there during a birthday party, he decided to teach them manners.

Mr. Vale: "You'll understand tomorrow."

Neighbor: "Understand what?"

Mr. Vale: "Respect."

The next morning, his truck sat sideways across their driveway. The family had a medical appointment at nine.

Neighbor: "Please move your truck."

Mr. Vale: "Maybe next time you won't take my space."

Neighbor: "The street is public. The driveway is not."

He folded his arms. The neighbor made one call. The tow truck arrived in eighteen minutes, followed by a community officer who asked whether Mr. Vale understood the difference between curb parking and blocking access.

He understood it best after paying the release fee.
