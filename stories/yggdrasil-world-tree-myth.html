The Existing World Tree Image

The existing story can be stated without adding anything new: Yggdrasil is the immense world tree in Norse cosmology. The tree connects or holds the realms of gods, humans, giants, and the dead. Norns, creatures, roots, wells, and cosmic tension appear around the tree in major summaries. Yggdrasil is linked to both the structure of the cosmos and the pressure of fate.

Yggdrasil World Tree belongs to Norse mythology. Its main value is the recognizable shape of the story, not a claim that every mythic event should be read as literal history.

Reported Cosmology Variants

Reported Variant: Lists and descriptions of the nine realms vary in later summaries and retellings. This belongs to Norse mythology reference tradition, so it should not be treated as the only or original version. Some sources emphasize the tree as axis of worlds, while others emphasize fate, roots, wells, and creatures. This belongs to Eddic and later summary traditions, so it should not be treated as the only or original version.

The circulation level is widely-known. That means the article can describe the tradition confidently, while still marking local, textual, or later differences as variants.

Mythic Context

Yggdrasil World Tree is most useful when read through role, symbol, and tradition rather than through a single modern version.

Symbolic Reading of Connection and Fate

Editorial Interpretation: Yggdrasil may be read as a symbol of connection under stress. The tree can suggest that cosmic order is alive, vulnerable, and constantly maintained.

This interpretation stays abstract. It explains meaning, symbol, and cultural force, but it does not create a new plot, new witness, new ritual, or new ending.

What the Sources Can Support
