FICTIONAL DIALOGUE DRAMA - NEIGHBOR DISPUTES

Board President's Daughter Took The New Tenant's Delivery Shelf. The Package Locker Log Put Her Name On It.

The board president's daughter treated the lobby shelf like her private storage. The new tenant tried to stay polite until a missing medicine delivery made the log unavoidable.

Published July 1, 2026 - 16 min read - Fictional dialogue-format drama

Fiction notice: This is an original fictional dialogue drama created for entertainment purposes. All characters, buildings, messages, places, and events are invented.

Move-In Week

New Single Mother Tenant: Hello. I moved into 3B yesterday. Is the lower delivery shelf assigned?

Building Manager: Welcome. Lower shelf is shared, but residents should collect same day.

Board President's Daughter: Shared is a generous word. Some of us have been here long enough to know how the lobby works.

New Single Mother Tenant: I understand. I only asked because I have a small child and some heavy deliveries coming.

Board President's Daughter: Everyone has a reason.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: The lower shelf is for whoever has a delivery. That is the rule.

Board President's Daughter: Thank you for the history lesson.

Building Manager: Let us keep the delivery shelf clear and fair.

Package Locker Log: 3B account activated. Lobby access enabled.

New Single Mother Tenant: Thank you. I will pick up quickly.

[The new tenant reads the last reply twice before putting her phone down.]

The First Box

Package Locker Log: 10:14 AM - Delivery scanned for Unit 3B. Lower shelf slot L2.

New Single Mother Tenant: My diaper box is not on L2. Did anyone move it?

Board President's Daughter: If it was huge, it was probably blocking the entrance.

New Single Mother Tenant: It says delivered to the shelf. I came down within twenty minutes.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: I saw a large box near the bench earlier.

Board President's Daughter: The bench is not storage either.

Building Manager: Please do not move packages unless they are blocking an emergency path.

Board President's Daughter: Then people should not order warehouse-sized boxes to a boutique lobby.

New Single Mother Tenant: I found it beside the recycling room. The box was open, but the contents are there.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: That is not okay.

Board President's Daughter: Nothing was missing, apparently.

[The conversation pauses, but the pressure stays in the room.]

The Second Reminder

Building Manager: Reminder: residents may not relocate another unit's delivery without approval.

Board President's Daughter: Reminder: residents may not turn the lobby into a childcare warehouse.

New Single Mother Tenant: I am trying to avoid conflict. Please do not move my deliveries.

Board President's Daughter: Conflict usually follows people who announce they are avoiding it.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: She is asking politely.

Board President's Daughter: You always defend the newest sob story.

New Single Mother Tenant: That is unfair.

Board President's Daughter: Unfair is my father spending years keeping this building nice and newcomers acting like rules are optional.

Building Manager: Your father's role on the board does not change package policy.

Board President's Daughter: We will see what the board says.

[The new tenant stops typing with one hand on the stroller handle.]

Private Pressure

Board President's Daughter: Private thread: You should be careful about making public complaints your first week.

New Single Mother Tenant: I only asked where my box went.

Board President's Daughter: And made everyone uncomfortable.

New Single Mother Tenant: My box was opened.

Board President's Daughter: You found it, yes?

New Single Mother Tenant: That does not make it okay.

Board President's Daughter: You rent here. My family practically built the rules here.

New Single Mother Tenant: I signed the lease and paid the same package fee.

Board President's Daughter: Then act like you want to stay.

[The new tenant screenshots the message before her thumb can shake too much.]

The Medicine Delivery

Package Locker Log: 2:06 PM - Temperature-sensitive delivery scanned for Unit 3B. Upper secure locker U4.

New Single Mother Tenant: My child's medicine delivery says U4, but U4 is empty.

Building Manager: I am checking the locker dashboard.

Board President's Daughter: Maybe check whether you entered the wrong code before accusing the building again.

New Single Mother Tenant: I entered the code from the delivery notice.

Board President's Daughter: People misuse systems and then blame residents who know how things work.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: I saw someone open U4 about ten minutes after delivery.

Board President's Daughter: Did you also see the moon landing?

Building Manager: Enough.

New Single Mother Tenant: I need that package today.

[The new tenant's message sits alone for almost a minute.]

In The Lobby

Board President's Daughter: I am in the lobby now. There is no medicine package here.

New Single Mother Tenant: I am also in the lobby.

Board President's Daughter: Then ask quietly instead of performing in the building thread.

New Single Mother Tenant: Please do not talk to me like that.

Board President's Daughter: Please do not bring chaos to a building that was peaceful before you arrived.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: She has a child waiting upstairs.

Board President's Daughter: And I have a father on the board. Everyone has someone.

Building Manager: I am five minutes away from the lobby.

Board President's Daughter: Good. Maybe explain to her that not every inconvenience is a conspiracy.

New Single Mother Tenant: I just want the package.

[The lobby door opens and closes twice. No one speaks first.]

The Dashboard Opens

Building Manager: I am opening the locker access record.

Board President's Daughter: Excellent. Please do.

New Single Mother Tenant: Can it show who opened U4?

Building Manager: It shows account, time, and camera timestamp. Not package contents.

Board President's Daughter: So it shows nothing useful.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: It shows who opened the locker.

Board President's Daughter: Thank you, detective.

Building Manager: Stop.

Board President's Daughter: I am allowed to defend myself.

Building Manager: You have not been accused by me. Yet.

[The board president's daughter stops smiling.]

The Locker Log

[The manager turns the tablet so everyone at the lobby desk can see the record.]

Package Locker Log: 2:06 PM - Unit 3B delivery placed in U4 by courier.

Package Locker Log: 2:14 PM - U4 opened with Unit 7A resident access.

Building Manager: Unit 7A is your unit.

Board President's Daughter: I opened the wrong locker by accident.

New Single Mother Tenant: Where is the package?

Package Locker Log: 2:15 PM - U4 door held open for 31 seconds. Item removed.

Board President's Daughter: I thought it was mine.

Building Manager: The label was for 3B.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: I saw the blue pharmacy bag in your tote.

Board President's Daughter: Why are you watching my tote?

New Single Mother Tenant: Please give it back.

[The board president's daughter looks toward the front windows before opening her tote.]

The Shelf Is Not Hers

Board President's Daughter: Here. It was a mistake.

New Single Mother Tenant: The cold pack is warm.

Building Manager: I will file a building incident report and contact the pharmacy about replacement instructions.

Board President's Daughter: An incident report? For a mistake?

Building Manager: For opening another tenant's secure locker, removing a labeled package, and denying it in the lobby.

Board President's Daughter: You are making this sound criminal.

Building Manager: I am making it sound documented.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: Those are different only when the record is kind to you.

Board President's Daughter: My father will hear about this.

Building Manager: He will receive the same incident summary as the rest of the board.

New Single Mother Tenant: I need to know if the medicine is still usable.

Building Manager: We will cover the replacement delivery fee if the pharmacy requires one.

[The new tenant holds the pharmacy bag with both hands, careful not to cry in the lobby.]

The Same Lobby

Package Locker Log: 3:02 PM - Unit 3B assigned reserved secure locker R1 for medical deliveries.

Board President's Daughter: She gets a reserved locker now?

Building Manager: For documented medical deliveries. Yes.

Board President's Daughter: So I lose access because she overreacted?

Building Manager: You lose shared-shelf privileges for thirty days because you removed another tenant's package.

Board President's Daughter: You cannot do that.

Building Manager: Policy 4.2 says I can after a documented locker violation.

Retired Hallway Neighbor: That policy was your father's proposal.

[The board president's daughter looks at the lobby notice board, then at the tablet, then at the people waiting behind her.]

New Single Mother Tenant: I do not want a fight. I just want my deliveries to stay where they are placed.

Building Manager: That is exactly what will happen.

Package Locker Log: 3:05 PM - Replacement delivery requested. Fee waived by building office.

Board President's Daughter: This is humiliating.

Building Manager: It was humiliating when you did it to her. This is the record catching up.

[The new tenant receives the reserved locker card at the same desk where she had been accused of causing trouble.]

[The board president's daughter stands beside the delivery shelf she can no longer use, holding an empty designer tote.]
