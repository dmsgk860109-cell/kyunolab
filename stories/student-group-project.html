Student Took Credit For The Whole Project, Then The Presentation Clicker Betrayed Him

He wanted solo applause. The speaker notes had saved every contributor's name.

Published July 1, 2026 · 7 min read

Fiction notice: This story is fictional and written for entertainment.

Dylan introduced the presentation with a smile that looked rehearsed in a mirror.

Dylan: "I handled most of the research and design."

Professor: "Most?"

Dylan: "Basically all."

He clicked to the next slide. Instead of the chart, the presenter view flashed onto the projector. In the notes field, every section was labeled with the name of the person who wrote it. Dylan's name appeared once: "Add conclusion if he responds."

The room read faster than he could click away.

Professor: "Please go back."

Dylan: "That was an old draft."

Teammate: "It was exported this morning."

The professor thanked the group, asked Dylan to sit down, and invited the other three students to present the work they had actually done.
