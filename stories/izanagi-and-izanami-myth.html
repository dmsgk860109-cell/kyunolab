The Existing Creation and Yomi Story

The existing story can be stated without adding anything new: Izanagi and Izanami are primordial Shinto deities. They create the islands of Japan and give birth to many kami. Izanami dies after giving birth to the fire god Kagutsuchi. Izanagi follows her to Yomi, breaks the boundary by looking at her, escapes, and purifies himself.

Izanagi and Izanami belongs to Japanese Shinto mythology. Its main value is the recognizable shape of the story, not a claim that every mythic event should be read as literal history.

Reported Source Variants

Reported Variant: The Kojiki, Nihon Shoki, and later summaries preserve differences in emphasis and detail. This belongs to Japanese myth sources and reference summaries, so it should not be treated as the only or original version.

The circulation level is widely-known. That means the article can describe the tradition confidently, while still marking local, textual, or later differences as variants.

Mythic Context

Izanagi and Izanami is most useful when read through role, symbol, and tradition rather than through a single modern version.

Symbolic Reading of Boundary and Purification

Editorial Interpretation: The myth may be read as a boundary story about creation, death, pollution, and purification. The failed return from Yomi can be interpreted as a mythic explanation of the separation between living and dead.

This interpretation stays abstract. It explains meaning, symbol, and cultural force, but it does not create a new plot, new witness, new ritual, or new ending.
