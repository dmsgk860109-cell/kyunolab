# Kyunolab Canonical Archive Mode

Canonical Archive Mode is an additional Kyunolab generation mode for well-known legends, folklore, myths, mysteries, and strange records.

It does not replace Original Archive Mode.

Original Archive Mode remains unchanged and continues to be used for newly imagined Kyunolab-style archive stories.

## Purpose

Use Canonical Archive Mode when the subject already exists in public cultural memory.

The goal is to write a Kyunolab archive article about a known topic, not to invent a new legend.

Canonical Mode should:

- preserve Kyunolab's calm, literary, mysterious archive tone
- explain why the subject continues to be remembered
- separate documented information from folklore, rumor, and interpretation
- avoid encyclopedia-style flattening
- avoid presenting uncertain claims as fact

## Subject Selection

Prioritize subjects such as:

- widely known urban legends
- famous ghost stories
- legends
- folklore
- myths
- unexplained mysteries
- supernatural traditions
- internet legends
- historically well-known mysteries

Example subjects include:

- Atlantis
- Excalibur
- Bigfoot
- Yeti
- Bloody Mary
- Slender Man
- Mothman
- Black-Eyed Children
- Area 51
- Bermuda Triangle
- Dyatlov Pass
- Roanoke Colony

This list is illustrative, not limiting.

## Research Requirement

Canonical Mode requires source review before drafting.

Before writing, consult at least 3 reliable sources.

Acceptable source types include:

- reputable reference works
- museum, university, library, or archive pages
- books or scholarly sources when available
- established folklore collections
- credible journalism for modern or historical mysteries
- primary source material when appropriate

Do not rely only on unsourced internet rumor, social posts, or copied summaries.

## Source Handling Rules

Only describe as factual what is supported across reliable sources.

If versions differ, do not invent one unified version. Say that the story appears in different forms across regions, retellings, or traditions.

Use cautious language for uncertain material:

- in many versions
- according to some retellings
- one common form of the legend says
- later versions often add
- the historical record is limited
- this is best understood as folklore rather than verified fact

Avoid fake certainty:

- this really happened
- the real truth is
- historians proved the ghost was
- science confirmed the legend
- the official explanation is hiding

## Required Article Elements

Every Canonical Mode article should include:

1. Introduction
2. Core story or best-known version
3. Historical background or tradition
4. Variants, regional versions, or later retellings when relevant
5. Why the story is remembered
6. What can be checked and what remains folklore
7. Kyunolab viewpoint
8. Story & Source Note
9. FAQ
10. Related Articles

## Writing Direction

Write in the existing Kyunolab style:

- calm
- source-aware
- readable
- mysterious without exaggeration
- literary without becoming vague
- concrete enough for search readers

Do not write like Wikipedia.

Do not write like a shock-horror blog.

Do not write like a conspiracy summary.

The article should help a reader understand:

- what the legend or mystery is
- where it is usually said to come from
- how the story changed over time
- what people often misunderstand
- why it still has cultural force

## Metadata Requirements

Set `generationMode` to:

```yaml
generationMode: canonical-archive
```

Canonical Mode articles should also define:

- `sourceStatus`
- `sourceNotes`
- `researchSources`
- `primaryKeyword`
- `secondaryKeywords`
- `relatedKeywords`
- `contentDNA.evidencePosture`

Recommended source statuses:

- Documented folklore / Multiple retellings
- Mythological tradition / Cultural folklore
- Historical mystery / Documented record with later folklore
- Internet folklore / Known fictional or community origin
- Local legend / Unverified reports

## Source Notes

Keep source notes concise but clear.

The Story & Source Note should say whether the article is based on:

- public-domain tradition
- documented folklore
- mythological material
- historical records
- modern retellings
- internet folklore
- speculative interpretation

If the subject includes both historical records and later legend, name that split clearly.

## Difference From Original Archive Mode

Original Archive Mode:

- creates new Kyunolab-style fictional or fictionalized archive stories
- may invent a new legend pattern
- still follows source-awareness and fact-safety rules

Canonical Archive Mode:

- does not invent new legends
- starts from an existing known topic
- researches before writing
- preserves uncertainty and variants
- uses source review as a required first step

The two modes are independent. Do not change Original Archive Mode prompts, templates, or behavior when using Canonical Mode.
