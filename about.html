About

A quiet archive for legends, folklore, mysteries, and strange tales.

The Strange Archive is a story publication by Kyuno Lab, dedicated to the odd stories people keep retelling.

We collect and shape entries about urban legends, internet folklore, classic tales, mythic creatures, strange places, and unexplained mysteries. The tone is curious, careful, and atmospheric rather than sensational.

Some entries may draw from documented traditions or public-domain material. Others are modern retellings, speculative interpretations, or original works inspired by folklore. We avoid presenting rumors as verified fact unless reliable sources support them.

What this archive is for

The Strange Archive is designed for readers who enjoy the shape of a story but still want clear boundaries around evidence. A record may be memorable, culturally important, or useful for understanding folklore without being presented as a confirmed event.

When a subject is uncertain, the article should say so plainly. The archive favors careful phrases, source notes, and visible limits over dramatic certainty. Mystery is allowed to remain mysterious; it does not need to be exaggerated to be worth reading.

How Kyuno Lab approaches strange material

Kyuno Lab treats The Strange Archive as a calm publication, not a claim factory. The site can discuss legends, folklore, mysteries, strange tales, and creative retellings while still separating documented history, repeated tradition, modern rumor, and original interpretation.

Readers should be able to tell when a page is explaining a known tradition, comparing versions, describing an internet-era motif, or preserving an original fiction-inspired record. That distinction is part of the site identity.
